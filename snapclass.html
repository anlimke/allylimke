SnapClass Design & Development

Our lab, Game2Learn, has a partnership with a local middle school. You can often find us in the classrooms there to support students and teachers during programming activities. This school primarily teaches students to code using the block-based and visual language, Snap! We know from this partnership, that it takes a lot for teachers to orchestrate programming activities in their classrooms. So, we developed SnapClass to help teachers create, assign, lead, and assess activities.

Understanding Teacher Needs

Participatory Design Sessions

We conducted participatory design sessions with 3 middle school teachers. Through these design sessions, we learned about teacher needs and created prototypes for SnapClass.

Table above shows teacher needs and struggles found through performing open-coding on design session transcripts.

Resulting Prototypes

The teachers each chose a need that was most important to them. During a live prototyping session, our team helped them design solutions for those needs.

Survey Findings

While the participatory design results gave us in depth knowledge of three teachers' needs, we wanted to know about the needs of a larger K-12 sample. Therefore, we deployed a survey to 39 K-12 computing teachers.

Recent Development Updates

We have been hard at work preparing SnapClass for classroom use. Here are some features that have recently been added to SnapClass recently.

  • An inclusive emoji library that students can use in their visual programs
  • An additional programming environment, Cellular
  • A coding gallery to allow students to post and react to snapshots from their peers
  • Teachers can send code snippets to students
